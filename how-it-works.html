The mechanic

Three quiet steps.

Nothing is made until enough people say yes. Nothing is wasted. A real person, a real recipe.

Step one

A baker opens a batch.

She proposes what she wants to make this week. Her recipe, her price, her pickup date. Every ingredient and allergen listed.

A proposal, not a product. Nothing on a shelf, nothing made yet.

Step two

You say yes.

Before anything is made. Your payment is held, never taken, until the kitchen opens.

If enough people don't commit before the deadline, every Franc returns to you. Automatically. Always.

Step three

The kitchen opens.

When enough neighbours have said yes, she turns on the oven. The threshold is set by the baker, never by us. Real ingredients, a real recipe, a real person.

You collect at the agreed time. You meet, briefly, the person who made your food. That is not a limitation. It is the point.

Where you collect

A small secret, kept on purpose.

Pickup happens at a place we keep private. Central Zürich, quiet, warm, and considered. The address is shared only once you've committed to a batch.

Something quietly held becomes something worth arriving for.
