The mechanic

Imagine Kickstarter. For homemade food. From your neighbour.

Nothing is made until enough people say yes. Nothing is wasted. No shelves, no guesswork, no anonymous production. Just a real person, a real recipe, and five neighbours who asked for it.

01

A producer proposes a batch.

A home baker opens a listing. Her recipe. Her story. Her price. A pickup date she chooses. She writes the ingredients — everything, including allergens — and sets the minimum threshold. Most batches need five people.

Once the listing is open, the clock starts. If five neighbours say yes before the deadline, the batch is confirmed. If not, it closes without a single item being baked.

5 people · minimum threshold Allergens · always declared
Lia proposes her Sicilian orange marmalade. Six 230g jars. CHF 14. Pickup Saturday.
02

You say yes before they cook.

You pay in advance. Your payment is held — it is not charged until the batch is confirmed. You are never charged for a batch that doesn't happen.

When five people have committed, the batch is confirmed. Lia gets the notification and turns on the oven.

If five people don't sign up before the deadline, every Franken returns to every person who said yes. Automatically. No questions, no waiting, no "credit on your next order".

Automatic full refund · always No charge until confirmed
03

You pick it up. You meet them.

Pickup is near the producer's home, at a time you both agree on. Typically ten minutes. You meet the person who made your food. You see where it was made.

That is not a limitation. That is the point.

The pickup is the moment that makes solo.quando different from every other food platform. It is a transaction, yes. But it is also a connection between two people in the same neighbourhood who would otherwise never have met.

~10 min · near the producer Agreed time · between you and them

Common questions

Everything you want to know before your first yes.

What happens if a batch doesn't reach five people?

Every payment is returned automatically to every person who committed. No exceptions, no credits, no partial refunds. The full amount comes back. Every time.

How do I know the food is safe?

Every listing on solo.quando must declare every ingredient and every allergen before it is published. Swiss food-safety law applies to all producers. We are also working with the Canton of Zürich's Innovation Sandbox to formalise the regulatory framework for home-baker platforms — so the rules are developed with us, not around us.

What does a batch typically cost?

Most batches on solo.quando fall between CHF 12 and CHF 40, depending on the item, quantity, and the baker's own pricing. The platform fee is 11–12%, which is deducted from the baker's payout. Buyers see the full price when they commit — no hidden charges.

How far away is pickup, typically?

Pickup is near the producer's home. In practice, most pickups are within a 15-minute walk or bike ride for buyers in the same Quartier. We match batches to buyers in the same neighbourhood — Zürich first, then other Swiss cities.

Can I order for someone else — as a gift?

Yes. You commit on behalf of the recipient, and the pickup coordination is arranged between the recipient and the producer. A handwritten note can be added. It makes an extraordinary gift.

When do the first batches open?
