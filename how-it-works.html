How it works

How it works.

Three simple steps. Pre-order before the kitchen opens. Pick up when it does.

01

Step one

A baker opens a batch.

She proposes what she wants to make this week. Her recipe, her price, her pickup date. Every ingredient and every allergen, listed.

A proposal, not a product. Nothing on a shelf, nothing made yet.

02

Step two

You say yes.

Before anything is made. You pay when you reserve, knowing the food only happens if enough people commit.

If the threshold isn't reached, every Franc returns to you. Automatically. Within 48 hours.

03

Step three

The kitchen opens.

The baker turns on the oven. She sets the threshold, never us. Real ingredients, a real recipe, a real person.

You collect at the agreed time, in central Zürich. You meet, briefly, the person who made your food.

Where you collect

A small secret, kept on purpose.

Pickup happens at a place we keep private. Central Zürich, quiet, warm, and considered.

The address is shared only once you've committed to a batch.

"Something quietly held becomes something worth arriving for."

Who this is for

Two kinds of people. One neighbourhood.

If you want to eat well

  • You want honest food made by hand, not a factory
  • You have tasted something homemade and never found it again
  • You would rather support your neighbour than a brand
  • You want to know exactly who made your food and why

If you want to bake more

  • The grandmother whose recipes should not be lost
  • The immigrant whose food does not appear on any Swiss shelf
  • The weekend baker who already has the people, just does not know it yet
  • The maker whose kitchen is their best asset
