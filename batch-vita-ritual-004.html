The story

From a tea house in Uji, to a kitchen in Zürich.

Anca recently came back from Japan with something she couldn’t stop thinking about. Not a souvenir. A ritual.

The way people there reach for matcha instead of a fifth coffee. Steady, calm, no crash. She brought some ceremonial matcha home with her and started making something around it — small daily rituals for busy lives.

That is how the VITA.RITUAL Daily Box was born. Six handmade balls. No refined sugar. Real ingredients, in small batches, made only when enough people reserve.

“I want this to feel like a small ritual, not a snack. Something you keep for yourself, on the days that ask for it.” — Anca

The matcha

Morihan, Uji. Since 1836.

The matcha in these balls is not a generic powder. It is from Morihan (森半), a tea house in Uji, Kyoto, founded in 1836. Nearly two hundred years of growing, picking, and stone-grinding tea in the region considered the home of Japanese matcha.

Morihan is the brand of Kyoeiseicha Co., Ltd. — a vertically integrated producer that grows, processes, and packages its own tea. Twice winner of the National Tea Appraisal and Tasting Competition in Japan.

Anca brought a batch back personally. This is what is in your matcha balls.
