The story

From a tea house in Uji, to a kitchen in Zürich.

Everyone knows the Japanese work ethic is legendary. They are incredibly disciplined — but what I didn't expect to see was how they manage the fatigue that comes with that.

They don't reach for a fifth coffee or a sugary energy drink when they hit a wall. They reach for matcha. Quiet, steady, no crash.

I was so moved by the power of it that I brought a batch home with me. Real matcha — from Morihan, a tea house in Uji, Kyoto, that has been making tea since 1836.

And I'm turning it into something I'm calling The Magic 3. Six small balls. Real ingredients. Zero sugar. Made by hand, only when you want them.

— Anca

The matcha

Morihan, Uji, since 1836.

The matcha in these balls is not a generic powder. It's from Morihan (森半), a tea house in Uji, Kyoto, founded in 1836 — nearly two hundred years of growing, picking, and stone-grinding tea in the region considered the home of Japanese matcha.

Morihan is the brand of Kyoeiseicha Co., Ltd. — a vertically integrated producer that grows, processes, and packages its own tea. They are FSSC22000 certified and have twice won the National Tea Appraisal and Tasting Competition in Japan.

Anca brought a batch back personally. This is what's in your Matcha Ball.
