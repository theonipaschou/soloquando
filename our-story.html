Our story

It began in her kitchen.

My grandmother taught me to bake. Patience. Butter. Citrus peel. Time.

She never used a recipe book. She remembered everything by hand. She called me to the kitchen when something needed stirring, and that is how I learned.

She is no longer here. The recipes are. So I make them. Most Sundays.

"One Sunday I made twenty-four cookies. I ate two. The rest went to waste. That was the Sunday I knew something was wrong."

The bigger picture

The same kitchen,
in a thousand homes.

I started talking to people in Zürich. Friends. Colleagues. Neighbours. Strangers in cafés.

What I expected to find: a small private problem. What I found: it was everywhere.

A grandmother making börek every Friday for thirty years, giving most of it to neighbours who never asked for it. A father baking sourdough for twelve when only three would eat it.

We were all doing the same thing. Baking from love, alone, throwing away what would not fit.

Why this exists
