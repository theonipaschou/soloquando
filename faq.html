The basics
6 questions
What is solo.quando?

A demand-first pre-order marketplace for homemade food in Zürich. A baker proposes a batch. Neighbours pre-order. Nothing is made until enough people say yes.

Think Kickstarter, for homemade food. Made by your neighbour.

solo.quando is also a small group of people who said yes early. They get priority on every batch, invitations to small gatherings, and a quiet line into what we're working on. Join us here.

What does joining solo.quando get me?

Four things, while we're a pilot in Zürich.

Priority on every batch. You hear before anyone else, and you reserve before public release.

Small gatherings. Kitchen tastings, supper-club style evenings, in Zürich.

Farm and producer visits. Where the strawberries grow, where the honey comes from, the people behind the ingredients.

The parts that never make it online. Recipe drafts, behind-the-scenes, the first to know what's coming next.

It's free, while solo.quando is small. The early people get the most.

What is a "batch"?

A batch is a small round of baking proposed by one of our bakers. They decide the recipe, the price, the pickup window, and the minimum number of pre-orders they need before they'll cook. Once enough neighbours say yes, the batch is confirmed and the kitchen opens.

When does this launch?

First batches in Zürich are planned for May 2026. Join solo.quando and you'll be the first to hear when a baker in your neighbourhood opens a batch.

Where does solo.quando operate?

solo.quando is currently a pilot project in Zürich. We are intentionally starting small: a single city, a small group of bakers, one curated pickup point. Once the model is proven here, we'll expand to other Swiss cities. We are not in a hurry.

Do you have a mobile app?

Not yet. solo.quando is web-first, designed to work cleanly on phone browsers. An app may come later, when there's something for it to do.

For neighbours
8 questions
How do I pre-order a batch?

You browse open batches, choose one, and reserve a portion. Your payment is held but not charged until the batch is confirmed. If the threshold isn't met before the deadline, the hold is released automatically.

What if a batch doesn't reach its threshold?

Every payment returns automatically and in full. No exceptions, no credits, no partial refunds. The full amount, every time.

Can I cancel my pre-order?

You can cancel any time before the batch is confirmed. Once a batch is confirmed and the baker has started cooking, the order is final, the same way it would be at any small restaurant taking a private booking.

How do I know the food is fresh?

The whole model is built around freshness. Bakers don't cook anything until enough neighbours commit. You collect within hours of it being made, often the same day. There is no shelf, no warehouse, no waiting.

What if I have allergies or dietary restrictions?

Every listing must declare every ingredient and every major allergen before publication. Bakers must also disclose if their kitchen handles other allergens (cross-contamination risk). If a baker omits something or misdescribes a batch, write to us at hello@soloquando.ch within 24 hours of pickup.

If you have severe allergies, we strongly recommend reading every ingredient list carefully and contacting the baker through the platform with any specific questions before pre-ordering.

What if the food isn't what I expected?

If something is materially different from how it was described (wrong ingredients, spoiled, missing) write to hello@soloquando.ch within 24 hours of collection. We will investigate and resolve fairly.

For matters of personal taste, we ask for understanding. Homemade food is not factory food. Every batch is unique.

Can I request a custom batch from a baker?

Not yet. At launch, batches are proposed by bakers, not requested by neighbours. As the platform grows, we plan to allow direct requests, but only between people who already know each other through the platform.

Is there a minimum order size?

No. You reserve a single portion per batch. The minimum threshold applies to the batch as a whole, not to your individual order. One portion is always enough.

For bakers
8 questions
Who can bake on solo.quando?

Anyone with a home kitchen, a recipe they're proud of, and the willingness to share it. No commercial kitchen required, no minimum volumes, no business background needed.

What matters is consistency, care, and honesty about what you make.

Do I need a permit to sell homemade food in Switzerland?

For most home bakers operating at small volumes, no specific permit is required, but you must comply with general Swiss food-safety law (LMG SR 817.0 and LGV SR 817.02) and your cantonal food-safety authority's guidance.

We guide every baker through the relevant rules during onboarding. The most common requirements: clear ingredient and allergen labelling, basic kitchen hygiene, and accurate descriptions of what you sell.

Do I need food-safety training?

Not legally required at small volumes, but strongly recommended. We can point you to short, low-cost hygiene courses available in Zürich. The peace of mind is worth it.

How much does it cost me to use solo.quando?

For our first cohort of bakers in Zürich, the platform is free. Our long-term model is a small commission per confirmed batch (planned at 10 to 15%). No subscription, no listing fees, no fees if a batch doesn't reach its threshold.

How do I get paid?

Payments are processed through Payrexx, a Swiss payment platform. When a batch is confirmed and pickup is complete, your payout is transferred to your Swiss bank account. Refunds, if any, are automatic and don't affect your future payouts.

What if not enough people pre-order?

Then nothing happens. The batch closes. Nobody is charged. You haven't lost a Sunday or a gram of flour. You can re-list the same batch or try a different one.

Can I cancel a batch I've proposed?

You can cancel an open batch at any time before it reaches its threshold. After confirmation, cancellation should only happen for genuine emergencies. Repeated late cancellations risk being asked to leave the platform.

Who is liable if someone gets sick from my food?

Under Swiss law, the producer is responsible for the safety of the food they sell. solo.quando provides the platform but is not the manufacturer of the food.

We strongly recommend bakers consider basic food-product liability insurance (typically a few hundred CHF per year). We can recommend Swiss insurers who offer this.

Payments & pickup
5 questions
How do I pay?

Payments are processed by Payrexx, a Swiss payment platform. Accepted methods include TWINT, Visa, Mastercard, and Apple Pay.

Your payment is authorised but not charged when you pre-order. The charge happens only if the batch is confirmed. If not, the authorisation expires and no money moves.

Where do I pick up?

Pickup happens at a single, central Zürich address we curate. Quiet, warm, and considered. The address is shared with you only once you've committed to a batch.

We do not deliver. Pickup is part of the experience.

Why is the pickup location secret?

Something quietly held becomes something worth arriving for. We share it only with people who've committed.

What if the baker doesn't show up?

This shouldn't happen, but if it does, we refund your full payment immediately and the baker faces consequences on the platform, up to and including being asked to leave. Tell us at hello@soloquando.ch.

What happens if I miss my pickup window?

Each batch has a clearly stated pickup window (usually 2 to 3 hours on a specific day). If you miss it without arranging an alternative with the baker, the food may be forfeited. Pickup is a commitment.

Legal & safety
4 questions
Is solo.quando legal?

Yes. solo.quando operates within Swiss food-safety law (LMG SR 817.0 / LGV SR 817.02) and the Swiss Code of Obligations (CO/OR). We are the platform, not the producer. Bakers are responsible for the safety, declaration, and compliance of their own food.

How is food safety handled?

Every listing must declare every ingredient and every major allergen. Cantonal food-safety inspectors retain the right to inspect any home kitchen producing food for sale, including those operating through solo.quando. We cooperate fully with all such inspections.

We onboard every baker with a written agreement covering hygiene basics and accurate labelling.

How is my personal data handled?

Personal data is processed under the Swiss nDSG (Federal Act on Data Protection). We don't sell or share your data for marketing. See the Privacy Policy for full details.

Is this accessible for people with disabilities or chronic illness?

Intentionally so. The single, curated pickup point in central Zürich means short pre-arranged collections in one place, significantly easier than a market or a shop with multiple visits. We can also accommodate specific accessibility needs. Just let us know in advance.

How is solo.quando different from Too Good To Go?

Too Good To Go sells leftover restaurant or supermarket food at the end of the day, to reduce waste. solo.quando does the opposite: nothing is made until enough people have pre-ordered. The food is fresh, made by your neighbour, and only because you asked.

How is solo.quando different from a farmers' market?

A farmers' market requires the producer to bake speculatively, transport everything to the market, and sell what they can. solo.quando inverts that: the order comes first, then the cooking, then the pickup. Less waste, less guesswork, less anonymous.
